Aftershocks continue into March as another earthquake hits Columbia area

Months after a notable earthquake hit the Midlands, smaller quakes continue to shake the Columbia area. The latest was confirmed Wednesday in Kershaw County. A 2.2 magnitude earthquake was recorded near Elgin, according to the U.S. Geological Survey. It hit at 11:09 a.m., the South Carolina Emergency Management Division said. The earthquake, or aftershock, that was recorded at a depth of 3.6 kilometers, or about 2.2 miles, was the 14th in the Palmetto State since the start of the new year, according to the South Carolina Department of Natural Resources. Thirteen of the quakes have been in the Midlands. Anyone who felt the earthquake can report it to the USGS. This continues a recent trend of seismic activity. Prior to Wednesday, the most recent incident was less than a week earlier when a 1.8 magnitude quake was confirmed on March 3. In all, 20 earthquakes have hit the Columbia area since a 3.3 magnitude quake was recorded on Dec. 27, 2021, according to the South Carolina Department of Natural Resources. No major damage or injuries have been reported from any of the recent quakes. Earthquakes that register 2.5 magnitude or less often go unnoticed and are usually only recorded by a seismograph, according to Michigan Technological University.
